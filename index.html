About Geomorphology

The scientific study of Earth's landforms and the processes that shape them — from plate tectonics to river erosion.

Earth's Internal Structure

  • Inner Core
    Solid iron & nickel, ~5,700°C temperature.
  • Outer Core
    Liquid iron & nickel, generates Earth's magnetic field.
  • The Mantle
    Semi-solid magma; convection drives plate tectonics.
  • The Crust
    Continental (thick) and oceanic (thin) outer shell.

Geomorphic Processes

Forces that continuously wear down and build up Earth's surface.

🖱️ Drag to rotate the 3D Earth

Endogenic Processes (Internal)

Volcanism

Movement of molten rock (magma) onto the surface. Creates lava plateaus, volcanic cones, and geysers.

Diastrophism

Deformation of the Earth's crust by folding and faulting — responsible for mountain building (Orogeny).

Plate Tectonics

Interaction of rigid lithospheric plates explaining the structure of the Earth's crust and many geological phenomena.

Exogenic Processes (External)

Fluvial

Erosion & deposition by rivers. Forms: Valleys, Deltas, Alluvial Fans.

Glacial

Action of ice. Forms: Cirques, Moraines, U-shaped Valleys.

Aeolian

Action of wind. Forms: Sand Dunes, Loess, Mushroom Rocks.

Coastal

Wave & tide action. Forms: Cliffs, Sea Arches, Stacks, Spits.

Assam Geomorphology

Assam's unique geological features shaped by the Himalayan orogeny and the mighty Brahmaputra river system.

🌊
Brahmaputra Floodplains

The river is characterized by a braided channel pattern, frequent shifting, and severe floods that deposit fertile alluvium.

🌿
Kaziranga Landscape

Tall elephant grass, marshland, and tropical moist broadleaf forests formed by alluvial deposits over millennia.

⛰️
Mikir Hills & Barail Range

Ancient hills composed of Archaean rocks, contrasting with the young alluvial plains of the valley.
